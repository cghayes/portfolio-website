About Me

I have always had an overt passion for modern technology, problem solving, and creating things.

My interest in web development started around 2006 when I began digging into and editing the code of custom LiveJournal themes. At the time, I had no idea my hours of tinkering with that HTML and CSS was planting the seed for what I wanted to do as a life-long profession.

Today, I have a bachelor's degree in computer art and animation, and I recently completed my associate's degree in website programming. I am fully, ardently immersed in the vast and ever-evolving nature of this industry.

I am driven by constantly learning and challenging myself. It's important to me to see the impact of my work, and I want to take on projects that are user-centric and clearly benefit people.

These days, my time is primarily spent practicing my skills in JavaScript and learning additional languages, libraries, and frameworks as often as I can. Right now, Vue.js and Sass have worked themselves into my daily learning routine.
