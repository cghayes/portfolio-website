Bonsais [July 2020]

Bonsai is a Japanese expression with deep history and meaning. Though bonsai literally translates to "tray planting," there is true art and joy to be found in growing and caring for these tiny trees. It takes years to train a bonsai into the shape you want, which takes lots of patience and precision. Photographing them requires these traits, as well. Just as you train a bonsai tree, you must practice calmness and precision to get the perfect shot.

More on photographing bonsais:


"A black backdrop makes the tree "pop" out, whereas light colors provide a softer, more balanced look. In the Kokufu albums, black backdrops are used primarily for flowering trees, and occasionally to highlight the white bark of deciduous trees such as beech or the white deadwood of junipers. An off-white backdrop is used for the majority of the trees."

- A Guide to Photographing Bonsai and Kusamono
